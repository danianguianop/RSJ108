How to take good pictures on a phone...

Assembly time: about 1 hour
Awesome rating: 100% successful

Photography is a very important part of journalism, so itâ€™s crucial to know how to take good pictures. Although many times not everyone will have access to expensive cameras and other equipment. But that doesn't mean you can't start learning how to take pictures with the camera that's in your pocket!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A Phone
  • Good Lighting
  • A Subject
  • A Background That's Not Too Busy

  1. Go to settings and set camera at highest resolution

    Changing your camera to take pictures at the optimal quality will insure that your pictures come out looking more profesional.

  2. Find the subject you wish to photograph

    This is where you can start to play around before you take the actual piture. Find what angles and distances you want to take the picture from, if you want to go landscape or portrait, if everything is in proportion etc..

    Play around with this step and dont be afraid to go out of your comfort zone!.

  3. Keep your background clear of unwanted things

    Make sure your subject is centred, or in tune with the background so that the main focal point is always the subject and not what may be in the back.

  4. Make sure you have good ligthing

    Poor lighting can make a picture look unattractive, and in many times a phone camera wont pickup everything in a picture that is too dark or too light. So its best to find a place that already has good lighting.

  5. If needed use flash

    The flash is something that should be used sparingly.If you are in very dark lighting its not always recomended as it wont make the picture look very good. Flash is better used when needing to fill in shadows.
